About Me

I am a professional historian who specializes in the history of the Middle Ages. I am currently a student at the University of Pennsylvania's Full Stack Web Development Program. I have studied in the United States and in the United Kingdom, where I earned my doctorate degree in History in the summer of 2019.

In 2019, I was a postdoctoral research assistant at the Malbork Castle Museum, in Poland, in addition to serving as a part time lecturer in the department of History at Cardiff University, in Wales. I have recently been awarded a postdoctoral fellowship at Nicholas Copernicus University, in Torun, Poland, to commence in September of 2021.

My interests in coding and programming began last year, when I was exposed to the impressive research conducted by medieval historians using programming languages to create navigable, accessible editions of primary sources for the broader public and students to use. There is so much potential for creating new, innovative, exciting, and insightful tools for everyone.

Please feel free to visit my GitHub for recent projects, in addition to my Academia.edu page for a sample of my scholarly works. You can also visit my LinkedIn page
