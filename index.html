About Me

I am a professional historian, with interests in the Middle Ages. I have lived and studied in the United States, and Europe, where I earned my doctoral degree in 2019. My research has been published in international scholarly journals, collections of studies, and mostly concerning the topics of the medieval history of northeastern Europe. More recently, my eyes have turned toward programming and its seemingly infinite applications not only to the ways that we can best experience our own world, but also how we can better understand our past. I am currently a student at the University of Pennsylvania's Full Stack Web Development Program to gain the necessary skills to apply programming to our knowledge of the medieval world.

While the field of history still involves traditional skills and visiting the archives, particularly the history of the premodern world, coding and programming developments have left a considerable mark on how scholars do their work. Thanks to the digitization of archival manuscripts and documents, which provide access to scholars working all over the world, there remains a lot of work to do, and I am interested in applying my new skills to doing this work. Some of the basic projects I am working on can be found above using the navigation bar. I will be updating this portfolio with different tools and projects that I've created along with my colleagues and friends.
